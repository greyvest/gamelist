Fighting games aren't my specialty; guys live for these games, live and breathe them, and have very strong opinions about them. I'm not trying to pretend to be an expert, but this is my overview of the genre from a casual perspective. For me, fighting games have always been a really fun way to get friends together and play.

## Street Fighter
Fighting games didn't start with Street Fighter, but Street fFghter really is where you should start. They're fun, they're where special moves and iconic genre conventions started, and they're home to a cast of wonderful characters. 
- [[Street Fighter II]] is the most iconic, with all of it's arcade variations. Playing it in the [30th anniversary collection](https://store.steampowered.com/app/586200/Street_Fighter_30th_Anniversary_Collection/) can give you online access as well. You should absolutely play some of this, with a friend at a cabinet is even better. 
- [Street Fighter 6](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1364780/Street_Fighter_6/) is a good modern jumping in point, with a lot of positive reviews and an active online scene. 
- Among very dedicated fighting game fans, [Street Fighter III : Third Strike](https://store.steampowered.com/app/586200/Street_Fighter_30th_Anniversary_Collection/) is considered an all time great. 

## Mortal Kombat
Mortal Kombat is another Iconic fighting game, but it's hard to compare the early games on mechanical merits to Street fighter. Highly controversial and violent, it certainly juiced up the fighting game scene and got a lot of people interested from a shock-value perspective. 
- [Mortal Kombat 9](https://store.steampowered.com/app/237110/Mortal_Kombat_Komplete_Edition/) is considered the best of the franchise's console era by many, but most entries are considered solid fun. 
- [Mortal Kombat II](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3454980/Mortal_Kombat_Legacy_Kollection/) is probably the best classic one, but they're all packaged into a bundle that lets you try them out. These arcade era games are best encountered in an arcade with a friend. 

## Injustice
Injustice is, for the most part, a reskin of the modern Mortal Kombat games, developed by the same team ( NetherRealm ) and featuring a lot of similar gameplay elements. For my money, these are more fun than the Mortal Kombat games, and Injustice 1 has a very fun campaign with a good comic book story. I have a lot of nostalgia of playing these games at a friend's house
- [Injustice : Gods Among Us](https://store.steampowered.com/app/242700/Injustice_Gods_Among_Us_Ultimate_Edition/) is older but still fun
-  [Injustice 2](https://store.steampowered.com/app/627270/Injustice_2/) is much more recent and probably mechanically better. 

## Guilty Gear / Arc System Works
The flagship franchise of Arc System Works, Guilty Gear is at the top of the modern "Anime" fighting game scene. It's exceedingly stylish, very mechanically sound, and has a very dedicated fanbase. Arc System works also has another well liked series called Blazblue, and are consitently making great games for other franchises like Undernight, Persona, and the excellent Dragonball Fighterz. 
- [Guilty Gear Strive](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1384160/GUILTY_GEAR_STRIVE/?curator_clanid=33025239) is the latest Guilty Gear offering, and extremely good. 
- [Dragonball : FighterZ](https://store.steampowered.com/app/678950/DRAGON_BALL_FighterZ/) is awesome and captures Dragonball Z so well. 
- [Persona 4 Arena Ultimax](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1602010/Persona_4_Arena_Ultimax/?curator_clanid=33025239) if you're looking to throw down with Persona Characters

## Tekken
Tekken occupies a fighting game space that has kind of a sidestepping third dimension to it. These games are very cool, and looked insanely cool back in the day in arcades, but are probably very complicated to try and get into. Still worth a shot for a fun time. 
- [Tekken 7](https://store.steampowered.com/app/389730/TEKKEN_7/) is the most beloved of the Modern games, with a long tail of post launch support and balance.
- There's much debate on which of these games is the best, but search out Tekken 3, 5 or the Tag games and pursue what looks cool
## Marvel vs Capcom
While these games included a ton of Street Fighter Characters, they really defined their own aesthetic and, for many people, have the best aesthetic realization of Marvel characters in any medium. Lots of juggles, swapping team members in and out. and just awesome to look at. 
- Play [Marvel Vs Capcom 2](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2634890/MARVEL_vs_CAPCOM_Fighting_Collection_Arcade_Classics/) just to see how good the characters look
- [Ultimate Marvel vs Capcom 3](https://store.steampowered.com/app/357190/ULTIMATE_MARVEL_VS_CAPCOM_3/) seems like the modern choice, with what seems like a still active scene and positive reviews. 
## Super Smash Brothers
Everybody knows Smash; it's an iconic and super fun franchise that invites casual play and has somehow attracted an incredibly sweaty competitive scene. 
- Super Smash Bros. Melee is the most beloved, and is a defining game for many people. Still incredibly fun today. Find a Gamecube and have a night with friends playing this.
- [Super Smash Bros. Ultimate](https://www.nintendo.com/us/store/products/super-smash-bros-ultimate-switch/) is the current iteration, and is considered very good. If you're looking for the easiest one to get into, get into Ultimate and play with your friends. It's got so many characters as to have become a meme. 
- Brawl is also really enjoyable, and has a really good set of singe player missions that people still covet to this day.
- The Original Nintendo 64 game is still fun, and is interesting in it's historical context ( Iwata basically programmed this on the weekends! ), but shows it's age. Pikachu in a Party Hat rules. 

## Honorable Mentions
- [Skullgirls](https://store.steampowered.com/app/245170/Skullgirls_2nd_Encore/) is a smaller but beloved game, with great style and mechanics and so much personality. This one is really worth playing
- Soulcaliber is a long running 3D fighting game series that's always been a second place to Tekken, with a similar 3D style, but has distinguished itself with it's crossovers. In particular, Soul Caliber II had Link in it, and Soul Caliber IV Included Darth Vader and Yoda. 
- Virtua Fighter holds a lot of love in Japan. The best way to play it is actually inside of Yakuza Zero, which makes it way more fun, honestly. 
- [Killer Instinct](https://store.steampowered.com/app/577940/Killer_Instinct/) started as two cool, edgy arcade games and released a reboot in 2017. It's a very fun game, not really popular anymore and your experience online will probably be pretty rough, but fun. It's got a dinosaur!
- Dead or Alive is distinguished by it's very risque characters and it's Beach Volleyball Spinoffs 😂
- [Brawlhalla](https://store.steampowered.com/app/291550/Brawlhalla/) is very popular, especially among younger demographics, and is free to play so easy to try. 
- [Lethal League](https://store.steampowered.com/app/261180/Lethal_League/) is a very different kind of fighting game where you're not hitting eachother directly, but playing a kind of baseball/racketball situation. Very fun.
