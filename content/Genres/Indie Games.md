"Indie" is a term that's become almost impossible to define, but in the 2000s was pretty explicitly just a title for games that came from very small teams without publishing deals. My definition of Indie is largely what fits the ethos; not a strict team size or budget, but certainly with consideration for those factors. The following are what I think are the vitally important indie games. 

---

Big Indie Hits that everyone (should) know
- Stardew Valley
- Among Us
- Hotline Miami
- Braid
- Limbo
- Castle Crashers
- Balatro
- Undertale
- Fez
- Shovel Knight
- Hollow Knight
- The Binding of Isaac

## The real small stuff
This category can get impossibly hard to define, and will be by far the most incomplete and subjective. These games are mostly gonna be on itch.io or some other free website, never made money, and made for the love of the game. Some exposure to them is a great way to understand the amateur side of game dev.
