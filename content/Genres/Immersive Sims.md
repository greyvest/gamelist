Fighting games aren't my specialty; guys live for these games, live and breathe them, and have very strong opinions about them. I'm not trying to pretend to be an expert, but this is my overview of the genre from a casual perspective. For me, fighting games have always been a really fun way to get friends together and play.

## Bioshock
The brain child of Ken Levine and a lot of other incredible developers, Bioshock and Bioshock Infinite objectively stand as some of the best and most important games ever made. Brimming with style, informed themes, great stories with great twists, and very fun gameplay, they really are some of the best the medium has to offer. 
- [[BioShock]] is a defining game, it's a must play
- [[BioShock Infinite]] is a stellar sequel. It caters more to the shooter crowd, by design, and so contains less of the Immersive Sim, environmental combat and interaction that the original has, but is still very important and absolutely worth playing. It's DLC is also spectacular. 
- Bioshock 2 is also really good; it gets left out as a kind of red-headed step child, but succeeds in it's story and combat and gives more time to spend in the spectacular city of rapture. Shouldn't be overlooked, but not as vital as the other 2. 

## System Shock
System Shock is where Bioshock started. Creepy, cool, and very fun, these games are foundational building blocks that a lot of other games built on, not just Bioshock. 
- [[System Shock 2]] was directed by Ken Levine, at irrational games, prior to making Bioshock, and is extremely interesting as a prototype for Bioshock as well as just a cool game. 
- [System Shock]() is much more primative, but still cool and with a lot of the great interactivity that defined the genre.

## Prey
Prey began as a kind of weird shooter in 2006 that messed with gravity in fun ways. It then had a reveal trailer for Prey 2 that still looks super cool to this day, but that game was cancelled. 
Then, in 2017, Arkane Austin released a reboot that was utterly fantastic but seemed to have nothing to do with the original series at all. This is pound for pound some of the best immersive sim gameplay you can play, and modernized to feel fresh and easy to engage with. Where System shock can be clunky, this game is smooth, beautiful, and super fun.
- Play [[Prey (2017)]]
## Thief
The Thief games were what Looking Glass studio worked on after System Shock, and are super beloved among fans of the genre. Much more focused on Stealth than fighting, they give the player tons of tools and make them very conscious of the level they're in, down to the amount of light in a given space. 
- [Thief : The Dark Project](https://store.steampowered.com/app/211600/Thief_Gold_1999/) is a pure Immersive sim to it's core. It's somewhat hard to play these days, because you have to be very patient, but it's very fun. [Nightdive studios is remastering it very soon, which may be worth waiting for. ](https://store.steampowered.com/app/4200960/Thief_The_Dark_Project_Remastered/)
- [Thief 2 : The Metal Age](https://store.steampowered.com/app/211740/Thief_II_The_Metal_Age/) is a follow up that's well liked
- [Thief 3 : Deadly Shadows](https://store.steampowered.com/app/6980/Thief_Deadly_Shadows/) is a strange but very cool game. Something about the way this game looks and feels is just really interesting. 
- [Thief](https://store.steampowered.com/app/239160/Thief/) was a reboot in the 2010s that, while not great, was still fun to play through. 

## Deus Ex
Warren Spector took the reigns of his own studio at Ion Storm and created Deus Ex, a slick cyberpunk future game that remains kind of obtuse but very deep. These games were carried on for multiple sequels, and are incredibly influential. 
- [Deus Ex](https://store.steampowered.com/app/6910/Deus_Ex_Game_of_the_Year_Edition/) is the one to play to experience just how intricate these games are. 
- [Deus Ex : Human Revolution](https://store.steampowered.com/app/238010/Deus_Ex_Human_Revolution__Directors_Cut/) is a very well like reboot, but is less in the immersive sim tradition and more of a stealth action game. 
## Dishonored
Developed by the French Branch of Arkane ( Prey ), the Dishonored games are super fun and much lower-tech immersive sims, focusing on Voodoo magic powers and very French, anarchist-cookbook like weapons and gadgets. 
- [Dishonored](https://store.steampowered.com/app/205100/Dishonored/) is super fun to play. Blinking around and stabbing/knocking guards out is some of the most fun you'll have in the genre. Playing through multiple times with a violent/non-violent play style gives a lot of replayability and changes the story.
- [Dishonored 2](https://store.steampowered.com/app/403640/Dishonored_2/) is a worthy successor, with perhaps not as well refined of a campaign as the original. Some of the visuals in this game are totally astounding, though, and it's worth playing. 
## Honorable Mentions
- [Ultima Underworld ](https://www.gog.com/en/game/ultima_underworld_1_2) is where the Immersive Sim family tree really starts, if you're looking to really get to the roots of the genre.
- Arkane's other games, [Arx Fatalis](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1700/Arx_Fatalis/) and [Dark Messiah of Might and Magic](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2100/Dark_Messiah_of_Might__Magic/) present primative but cool ways to get more of the genre. Dark Messiah, in particular, has a kick button that people *love* to use to kick people off cliffs. That's good game design.
- [CTRL ALT EGO](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1571940/Ctrl_Alt_Ego/) is a recently popular indie immersive sim. 
- [Gloomwood](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1150760/Gloomwood/) is looking to be a spiritual successor to Thief by extremely dedicated fans.
